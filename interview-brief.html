Core thesis

The forward-deployed engineer closes the distance between model capability and operating ownership.

Actual mandate

Reduce the distance between a client’s AI ambition and a production-capable workflow without creating uncontrolled technical debt, governance exposure or permanent dependence on the delivery pod.

Operating tensions

Frontier speed vs. enterprise control

Prototype rapidly while designing permissions, evidence, economics, support and ownership from the start.

Client specificity vs. reusable IP

Fit the real workflow while retaining reusable code, evaluations, prompts, runbooks and lessons.

Model capability vs. system reliability

Move beyond impressive outputs toward repeated-run stability, recovery and maintainability.

Agent autonomy vs. human authority

Earn autonomy through consequence, evidence, permissions and reversibility.

Operating model

Frame → Prove → Bound → Embed → Transfer. No stage advances on model performance alone; the next operating obligation must be satisfied.

Five interview claims

  • Use-case selection is an engineering decision.
  • Evaluation must represent the workflow, not merely the model.
  • Human review is an architecture component.
  • Production begins before deployment through logging, versioning, testing, rollback, economics and ownership.
  • Every engagement should leave behind a client-owned workflow and reusable Deloitte delivery assets.

Strongest objection

Concern: Does broad operating and leadership experience translate into daily coding, testing, integration debugging, documentation and contribution inside an FDE pod?

Response: The concern is reasonable. My titles describe the scope of problems for which I have been accountable—not a preference to remain distant from the work. Judge the direct artifacts, technical reasoning, workflow logic, evaluation discipline and willingness to state exactly where production-platform evidence is not yet verified.

90-day thesis

Days 1–30: learn the pod, approved platforms and standards; ship the first reviewed contribution. Days 31–60: own one bounded AI-enabled workflow with realistic integration, authority and evaluation. Days 61–90: prove operability, client ownership and reusable delivery value.

  4. Where have client GenAI programs most commonly stalled after a successful prototype?
  5. What does excellent Associate FDE performance look like after six months?
