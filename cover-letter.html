Deloitte Frontier GenAI FDE Team,

Deloitte’s Forward Deployed Engineer role sits at the point where an impressive prototype either becomes part of the client’s operating system—or remains a demonstration. That boundary is where I have spent much of my career: translating complex technical possibilities into visible work, explicit decisions, reliable handoffs and measurable operating outcomes.

At Vape-Jet, I work directly across production, quality, support, engineering, ERP, telemetry, customer signals and a fielded robotics fleet to turn fragmented information into prioritized action, standard work and operating feedback loops. Through DudeWorth, I design agentic workflow patterns combining structured context, retrieval, AI agents, human judgment, escalation, decision records, evaluation and governance rails. At Amazon, I built operating mechanisms in a 24/7 environment supporting approximately five million second-day orders annually. Earlier, at Compunetics, I led technical programs, engineering, manufacturing and quality in advanced-electronics environments where failure modes, stakeholder clarity and process controls mattered.

That record does not make me a conventional career software engineer, and I would not present it as one. It does make me an operator-technologist who is comfortable entering ambiguous systems, working hands-on with software and workflows, making tradeoffs visible and building the mechanisms that allow emerging capabilities to survive real use. I am pursuing this role because I want to be close to the build and the client problem—not distant from them.

I created an independent candidate vision at https://russelldudek.github.io/deliotte/ to demonstrate how I would approach the mandate. It includes a working deployment-posture lab and a five-stage Forward Deployment Contract—Frame, Prove, Bound, Embed and Transfer—that makes human authority, evaluation, observability, economics and client ownership explicit before a workflow advances.

I would welcome the opportunity to discuss where Deloitte sees the greatest gap between successful GenAI prototypes and client-owned capability, and how my operating discipline, technical translation and hands-on workflow design could strengthen an FDE pod.

Carpe diem,
