Contract rule

No stage advances on model performance alone. A workflow advances only when its next operating obligation is satisfied.

01 · Frame

Obligation: Define the changed work, affected user, current failure mode, value hypothesis, baseline, constraints and accountable client owner.

Exit: The opportunity describes a workflow problem—not merely an AI feature request.

02 · Prove

Obligation: Build the smallest end-to-end implementation capable of testing context, tool use, model behavior, workflow fit and user usefulness.

Exit: A representative user can complete meaningful work with the capability.

03 · Bound

Obligation: Define tool permissions, human approvals, escalation, refusal, rollback, evaluation, versioning, cost, latency and model-risk limits.

Exit: Allowed, prohibited and held actions are explicit.

04 · Embed

Obligation: Integrate identity, APIs, workflow systems, logging, observability, CI/CD, support and operating cadence.

Exit: The capability works inside the client’s real workflow rather than beside it.

05 · Transfer

Obligation: Complete the runbook, ownership assignment, training, architecture record, reusable assets, backlog and performance-review cadence.

Exit: The client can operate, evaluate and improve the workflow without permanent dependence on its original builders.

What the contract prevents

  • A technically impressive demonstration with no named operating owner.
  • Human review added as a disclaimer rather than designed into system state and permissions.
  • Production concerns deferred until after architecture and interface decisions harden.
  • One-off client customization that leaves no reusable delivery asset or lesson.

Deployment posture dimensions

Business outcome

Changed decision or task, named user, baseline and evidence of value.

Quality and reliability

Representative cases, failure categories, repeated-run behavior and regression evidence.

Human authority

Approval, escalation, reversibility and consequential-action boundaries.

Economics and latency

Cost per completed workflow and response time appropriate to the work.

Operability

Logging, diagnosis, recovery, support and version visibility.

Transfer and reuse

Named owner, documentation, training, independent change and reusable assets.

Release postures

Assist inside the workflow

Human acts; AI retrieves, organizes, summarizes or drafts. Use when consequence or evidence does not support action authority.

Recommend, human decides

AI synthesizes evidence and recommends an action while the human retains final authority and the decision path is recorded.

Execute with approval

The system prepares or initiates consequential work only after explicit approval, with full traces and clear rollback.

Bounded autonomous execution

Low-consequence, stable, permission-limited action with monitoring, exception routing and automatic fallback.

Hold for redesign

Consequence, uncertainty, ownership or evidence does not support release. Narrow the action or strengthen the system before proceeding.

Transfer standard

The client team can operate, evaluate, diagnose and improve the workflow—and Deloitte retains a reusable pattern, evaluation or delivery lesson.
